Dr. Severo Ochoa

The Nobel who discovered the mechanisms in the biological synthesis of RNA and DNA

Here's a time line of Dr. Ochoa's life

  • 1908 - born in Luarca (Asturias, Spain).
  • 1923 - goes to the University of Madrid Medical School, where he hoped to work with Ram√≥n y Cajal.
  • 1927 - spends the summer of 1927 at University of Glasgow working with D. Noel Paton on creatine metabolism and improving his English skills.
  • 1929 - completes his undergraduate medical degree. His creatine and creatinine work led to an invitation to join Otto Meyerhof's laboratory at the Kaiser Wilhelm Institute for Biology in Berlin-Dahlem.
  • 1933 - returns to Madrid where he is Director of the Physiology Section at the University of Madrid Medical School.
  • 1936 - Spanish Civil War is erupted and Ochoa and his wife traveled to Germany. He returns to Meyerhof's Kaiser Wilhelm Institute for Biology.
  • 1938 - becames Demonstrator and Nuffield Research at the University of Oxford.
  • 1940 - starts to work at Washington University's School of Medicine.
  • 1942 - becames Research Associate at the New York University School of Medicine.
  • 1954 - becames Professor of Biochemistry and Chair of the Department Of Biochemistry.
  • 1957 - He is elected to both the US National Academy of Science and American Academy of Arts and Sciences<./li>
  • 1959 - Ochoa and Arthur Kornberg are awarded the Nobel Prize for Physiology and Medicine "for their discovery of the mechanisms in the biological synthesis of ribonucleic acid and deoxyribonucleic acid".
  • 1985 - returns to Spain as a science advisor.
  • 1993 - dies in Madrid (Spain) at the age of 88.
